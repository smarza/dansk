Tal 0–10

0–10
English: The base numbers from zero to ten. Learn these first — the bigger numbers are built from them.

Tal 11–20

11–20
English: The numbers eleven to twenty each have their own word. Note that 11 is elleve and 12 is tolv, while 13–19 end in -ten.

Tal 21–100

21–100
English: Danish builds these numbers as unit + og + ten. For example 21 is enogtyve (one-and-twenty) and 56 is seksoghalvtreds (six-and-fifty). The tens are: tyve (20), tredive (30), fyrre (40), halvtreds (50), tres (60), halvfjerds (70), firs (80), halvfems (90), hundrede (100).

Store tal · 21 til 1.000.000.000

21–1.000.000.000
English: Large numbers stack the building blocks: hundrede (hundred), tusind (thousand), million (million) and milliard (one billion). The word og (and) connects the last small part, for example et tusind to hundrede og fireogtredive = 1.234.
